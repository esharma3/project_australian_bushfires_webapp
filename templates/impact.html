Impact on the Economy

The 2019-2020 Australian bushfire seasons was one of the worst on record, in terms of total acres burned, the fires are in the top 6 worst in the history of the country. Nearly 46 million acres were burned resulting in the destruction of many of Australia's state and national parks.


Historic trends show that bushfire home destruction and loss of human life correlate on nearly every major bushfire event. The 2019 bushfire season was no exception and ranks very high in regard to total amount of human lives lost and homes destroyed.


Australian GDP/Billion US Dollars shows no major disruptions to Australians macro-economy during these large natural disasters.
