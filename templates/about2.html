  • September 2018

    Eastern Australia experiences its driest September ever amid a prolonged drought that began in 2017.

  • September 2019

    Australia's Weather Bureu predicts at least three more months of drought as fires begin to spark.

  • September - November 2019

    Australia experiences its driest Spring on record.

  • October 2019

    Australia's annual bushfire season officially begins. The prolonged drought causes embers from fires to spread, leading to more intense fires in the 2019 season. On October 26th, lightning bolt starts a mega fire in Gospers Mountain, NSW. It became the epicentre of the biggest forest fire to have started from a single ignition point that Australia has ever known, with 444,000 hectares destroyed. A bushfire in Port Macquarie, NSW, starts due to dry electrical storm. October 28th a fire near Cattai, NSW, claims a life and burns 12,000 hectares.

  • November 2019

    Three people die in fires in Queensland and New South Wales within the span of two days from November 8th to November 10th. A state of emergency is declared in New South Wales and Queensland on November 11th. November 12th a fire in Lane Cove National Park, NSW. A C-130 Air Tanker made several fire retardant drops, saving the suburb.

    November 20th, a fire breaks out in Yorke Peninsula, SA. It threatened the towns of Yorketown and Edithburgh. It destroyed at least 11 homes and was believed to have started from a sparking electrical transformer. It burnt about 5,000 hectares of land.

  • November - December 2019

    Fire continues to spread. For the first time ever, US firefighters are deployed to Australia on December 8th.

  • December 2019

    December 14th, fire moves to Southern Highlands, NSW. Firetrucks overrun by fire, and two firefighters killed. Australia experiences its hottest day in history with average temperatures reaching 105.6 F on December 18th. On December 21st, a bushfire in the East Gippsland region of Victoria begins creating its own weather. The smoke generates clouds that create thunderstorms ultimately leading to more fires.

    On December 26th lightning sparks fire in the Sterling Ranges, WA, tearing through more than 40,000 hectares of parkland.

  • December 2019 / January 2020

    Thousands are told to evacuate East Gippsland as the fire danger zone spreads on December 28th. Major roads close in East Gippsland 48 hours later, reportedly trapping the residents and visitors who didn't evacuate earlier. The fires continue to worsen. The fires have killed a total of at least 16 people by December 31st. By the next day, out of necessity, Australia opens 15 South Coast bushfire evacuation centers.

  • January 2020

    New South Wales Premier Gladys Berejiklian declares a state of emergency and Victorian Premier Daniel Andrews declares a state of disaster January 2nd. Next day, a devastating fire on Kangaroo Island, SA, burning 170,000 hectares. This represents a third of the island. Australian Navy evacuates Mallacoota, VIC, as bushfires hit the town.

    By January 23rd, the Snowy Mountains area is ablaze as the fires continue to spread south. The death toll rises to 23 two days later. Decreasing winds and temperatures in Victoria and New South Wales ease conditions, but fires are expected to continue burning for weeks. Three American firefighters die after their Lockheed C-130 Hercules plane crashed while battling bushfires in southern NSW. The total number if firefighter deaths this bushfire season to eight.
