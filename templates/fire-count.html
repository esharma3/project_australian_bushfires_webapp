Australian Bushfires Count (2002 - 2020)

New South Wales, Queensland, Victoria

Data Source: NASA MODIS Cumulative Active Fire Detection Data
For New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland, summed per ‘fire year’ running from July 1st to June 30th in the following year. The 2019 - 2020 fire year numbers are based on data collected till January 2020.

This fire year (July 2019 to Jan 2020), fire detections in New South Wales are significantly higher than the previous record year (the 2002-03 fire season). In Victoria, fires are above average at this point in 2019-2020 but have not yet surpassed previous extreme fire years in 2002-2003 and 2006-2007. In Queensland, fire counts were consistent with previous years, despite large wildfires in southern Queensland, since total fire activity in this state is dominated by savanna fires in northern Queensland, a natural part of these ecosystems.

Year 2019 - 2020 Bushfires at a glance

New South Wales was impacted the most from 2019-2020 fire year bushfires. Out of New South Wales, Queensland and Victoria, New South Wales had about 60% of bushfires, with Queensland having about 33% and Victoria having about 6% of bushfire incidents.

Cumulative Fire Count 2002 - 2020

A cumulative fire count of bushfire incidents from 2002 till 2020 shows that Queensland has had a significantly high number of bushfires - 1,115,302. New South Wales has had 315,022 bushfire incidents and Victoria has had 125,881 bushfire incidents during this period.

The graphs are based on the fire-year in Australia that begins from July 1st of every year and goes on till June 30th of the next year. The month of July shows almost none to very small number of fires but as the days progress the number of daily fires starts increasing. Based on these graphs, these fires show a sudden significant increase during the summer months from November till February before they become nearly constant and then start declining towards the end of the fire year.
